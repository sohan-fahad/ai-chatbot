Abstract
This project focuses on the design and development of a University Management System to streamline academic and administrative processes within a higher education institution. The system aims to automate student enrollment, course registration, grade management, faculty assignment, and administrative operations, reducing manual workload and improving efficiency. It supports multiple user roles including students, faculty members, department heads, and administrators, each with specific permissions and functionalities. The report includes comprehensive requirement analysis, use case diagrams, and an Entity-Relationship (ER) diagram to model the database structure. A user-friendly interface has been designed with robust input validation to ensure data accuracy and security. By integrating these components, the University Management System enhances operational transparency, facilitates better academic planning, and provides real-time access to critical information for all stakeholders.
________________


Introduction
University management refers to the coordination and administration of academic programs, student records, faculty assignments, and institutional resources within a higher education setting. It encompasses the systematic handling of admissions, course scheduling, examination management, grade recording, and communication between students, faculty, and administrative staff.
This system is essential for universities and colleges of all sizes, helping them maintain accurate records, streamline workflows, and provide better services to the academic community. By implementing a centralized and automated university management system, institutions can improve accessibility, reduce paperwork, enhance communication, and ensure compliance with academic standards.
The system supports multiple user roles, each with distinct responsibilities and access privileges. Effective monitoring of user activities and maintaining accurate information is crucial for the successful operation of such a system.
The goal of this report is to outline the design and development of an automated university management system that leverages modern technology to improve efficiency, provide real-time access to academic information, and ensure smooth coordination among students, faculty, and administrators throughout the academic lifecycle.
